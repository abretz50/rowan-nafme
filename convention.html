2026 NJMEA State Conference

The 2026 NJMEA State Conference will be held at the Atlantic City Convention Center February 19th-21st, 2026. The conference hotel is the Sheraton, which has an enclosed walkway that takes you directly to the Convention Center.

What to Expect

Join us for a weekend of more than 200 Professional Development Workshops and Performances! From Thursday February 19th through Saturday February 22nd our Chapter will be staying in Atlantic City for the 2026 NJMEA State Conference. Student who attend will participate in Professional Devlopments, attend lectures from leaders in our field, witness performances from statewide ensembles, and so much more! The networking, career skills, and insights gained from this conference have consistenly insighted a positive disposition in all our educators who have had the opportunity to attend.

Conference Schedule

Events and Experiences

Convention Center

The Atlantic City Convention Center is a beautiful facility that offers a convenient and comfortable experience for attendees. One of its most notable features is the attached Sheraton hotel, which provides easy access to accommodations for those attending the conference. The center also offers on-site parking, making it easy for visitors to arrive and depart. With its well-equipped and flexible conference rooms, the Atlantic City Convention Center has been a popular choice for NJMEA conferences for years. The center's emphasis on safety and security also gives us peace of mind, knowing that our attendees will be well taken care of! We cannot wait to be attending the 2026 NJMEA State Conference at this fantastic venue!

Accommodations

We will be staying at the Sheraton Atlantic City Convention Center Hotel, which is conveniently attached to the convention center. The hotel offers comfortable and accessible accommodations, with amenities such as a 24-hour fitness center, gift shops, and a ballroom. Each room can accommodate up to 4 people, and we will be splitting the cost of the room to make it more affordable. We will be assigning groups to share rooms, so be sure to let us know if you have any preferences or special requests. We're looking forward to a comfortable and convenient stay at the Sheraton!

Vendor Expo

The vendor expo is a fantastic opportunity for our students to explore the latest resources and technologies available to music educators. They'll learn about new products and services, discover innovative tools and materials, and gain a better understanding of how to maintain and care for their instruments. Our students will also have the chance to learn about summer development programs, network with other teachers and music professionals, and make connections that can help them in their future careers as music educators.

Professional Developments

At this conference, our students will have the opportunity to attend a wide range of professional developments, potentially doubling or even tripling the amount of presentations they would normally attend in an entire academic year. With a vast catalogue of presentations to choose from, students can select the sessions that interest them most, gaining valuable insights from a diverse range of experts, including new teachers, seasoned educators with decades of experience, collegiate professors, and even early education specialists. All of these professionals bring unique perspectives and knowledge to the table, and we encourage our students to make connections, ask questions, and learn from their experiences. By attending these professional developments, our students will gain career-changing knowledge and make valuable contacts that can benefit them long after they graduate.

Reading Sessions

Throughout the conference, students will have the opportunity to participate in numerous score reading sessions, where they can bring their instruments or sing, as appropriate, and experience new and emerging standards in their fields. These sessions provide a unique chance for students to sight-read music in an ensemble setting, working together with their peers to bring the scores to life. And as a special bonus, students will take home free copies of the music they've sight-read, allowing them to continue exploring and learning from the experience long after the conference is over. Whether you're an instrumentalist or a vocalist, these score reading sessions are a fantastic way to discover new repertoire, challenge yourself, and make music with others in a supportive and collaborative environment.

Performances

Throughout the conference, we'll have the opportunity to enjoy a wide range of performances by high school groups, colleges, and professional ensembles. These performances will be scheduled throughout the event and will be included on the events schedule, so be sure to check it out and plan your attendance. Take advantage of this opportunity to be inspired, learn from others, and appreciate the hard work and dedication that goes into creating great music.
