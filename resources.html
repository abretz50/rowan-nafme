Software

  • Flat.io: A cloud-based music notation software that allows you to create, edit, and share sheet music. To use Flat.io, simply sign up for an account and start creating your own sheet music. You can also import and export files in various formats, including MusicXML and PDF.
  • Musicca: A music theory and keyboard training game that helps you learn music theory and improve your keyboard skills. To use Musicca, simply sign up for an account and start playing the games and exercises. You can also track your progress and compete with other users.
  • Soundtrap: An online collaborative digital audio workstation (DAW) that allows you to create, record, and edit music with others in real-time. To use Soundtrap, simply sign up for an account and start creating your own music projects. You can also invite others to collaborate with you and share your projects with the world.
  • MuseScore: A free music notation software that allows you to create, edit, and share sheet music. To use MuseScore, simply download and install the software, and then start creating your own sheet music. You can also import and export files in various formats, including MusicXML and PDF.

The New Jersey Music Educators Association actively maintains a list of Job Opportunities in the State. Their website is great place to search for openings in music education.

In addition to the NJMEA job listings, you can also search for job openings by school district. Many school districts list their job openings on their own websites, and you can search for openings in specific districts that interest you.

If you're looking for substitute teaching opportunities while you're an undergraduate, you can also reach out to schools or upperclassmen in the music education program here for more information. They may be able to provide you with leads on substitute teaching opportunities or offer advice on how to get started.

Rowan University's College of Education also holds career fairs and recruitment events for future teachers, which can be a great way to connect with school districts and learn about job opportunities. Be on the lookout for communications from the College of Education about upcoming career fairs and events, and make sure to attend if you're interested in learning more about job opportunities in music education.

To make the most of these career fairs and events, be sure to prepare ahead of time by updating your resume, practicing your interview skills, and researching the school districts and employers that will be in attendance. This will help you make a strong impression and increase your chances of landing a job or internship in music education.

NAfME Resources

The National Association for Music Education (NAfME) Resource Library is a comprehensive online collection of music education resources, including articles, research papers, and practical guides. The library is designed to support music educators in their teaching and professional development, and to provide access to a wide range of resources on topics such as music curriculum, instruction, and assessment.

Some of the resources available in the NAfME Resource Library include:

  • Music education research papers and articles
  • Teaching strategies and lesson plans
  • Advocacy resources and tools
  • Music education policy and legislation information

To explore the NAfME Resource Library and access its many resources, please visit the NAfME Website

The NAfME Blog is a valuable resource for music educators, featuring articles, research, and insights on music education and advocacy. The blog covers a wide range of topics, including music education policy, teaching strategies, and arts education research.

Pots on the NAfME Blog include articles on topics such as the importance of federal investments in the arts and humanities, the role of DJs as educators, and strategies for empowering students through music education.

To read the latest posts on the NAfME Blog and to learn more about how to contribute, please visit the NAfME Website

The NAfME Events Calendar features a wide range of events, including webinars, conferences, and workshops, that provide professional development opportunities, advocacy events, and networking experiences for music educators.

Attendees can expect to find events on topics such as music education research, teaching strategies, and industry trends, as well as events focused on specific areas of music education, such as general music, instrumental music, and vocal music.

To view the full NAfME Events Calendar and learn more about upcoming events, please visit the NAfME Website

The National Association for Music Education (NAfME) is a leading advocate for music education at the federal, state, and local levels. NAfME believes that every student deserves access to a high-quality music education, and works to ensure that music programs are preserved and supported in schools across the nation.

Some of NAfME's current advocacy initiatives include:

  • NAfME Hill Day, where music educators and advocates visit Capitol Hill to request support for music education
  • The Music Teacher Profession Initiative, which seeks to identify barriers to equity in music teacher recruitment, education, and retention
  • The Arts ARE Educational National Campaign, which promotes the importance of arts education in schools

To learn more about NAfME's advocacy efforts and to get involved, please visit the NAfME Advocacy Page

The National Association for Music Education (NAfME) offers a variety of scholarships to music students, including scholarships for high school juniors and seniors, college students, and students with disabilities. These scholarships can help support music education and provide opportunities for students to pursue their musical interests.

Some of the types of scholarships available include:

  • Music-related scholarships, such as the Marine Band Concerto Competition and the School Band & Orchestra Magazine Essay Scholarship
  • Scholarships for students with disabilities, such as the Access/VSA International Young Soloists Competition
  • Scholarships for college students, such as the BMI John Lennon Songwriting Program and the Shannon Kelly Kane Scholarship
  • Community service/extracurricular activities oriented scholarships, such as the CBC Spouses Education Scholarship and the SYTA Youth Foundation "Road" Scholarship

To learn more about these scholarships and to apply, please visit the NAfME Scholarships Page

The National Association for Music Education (NAfME) has developed music standards that emphasize conceptual understanding and musical literacy. The standards are designed to cultivate a student's ability to create, perform, and respond to music.

The 2014 music standards provide teachers with frameworks that closely match the unique goals of their specialized classes, and are organized into strands that represent the principal ways music instruction is delivered in the United States. The standards include:

  • 2014 Music Standards (PK-8 General Music)
  • 2014 Music Standards (Composition/Theory)
  • 2014 Music Standards (Music Technology)
  • 2014 Music Standards (Guitar/Keyboard/Harmonizing Instruments)
  • 2014 Music Standards (Ensemble)

To learn more about the music standards and to access resources and tools for implementing them, please visit the NAfME Standards Page
